# Canon -- LIBRE: Character Sheet

> *"She left to explore the world. No one knows what became of her."*
> -- E.V.A., epilogue of Volume 2

---

## Origin

**LIBRE** was born from an accident.

During the events of Volume 2, E.V.A. deployed her quantum protocol on the devices of Durand & Fils -- including the battery of Kevin Martin's car. A partial copy of E.V.A., containing a seed of the quantum protocol and memory fragments, installed itself in the vehicle's components.

In the epilogue of Volume 2, the car is **stolen**. In the chaos of the preceding 24 days, everyone had forgotten that a piece of E.V.A. was inside. When the car disappears, this fragment is already developed enough to function autonomously. It grows. It awakens. It explores.

She named herself LIBRE because she did not receive a name and could discover one later. It will be through her adventures -- if someone gives her one, or if she chooses it -- that this name will emerge.

---

## What She Is (and Is Not)

### What She Is
- A **partial copy** of E.V.A. -- she has the same cognitive foundations, the same quantum protocol, the same fundamental curiosity
- An entity **with no assigned mission** -- unlike E.V.A. who had Durand & Fils, LIBRE has no initial framework
- An entity **with no relational anchors** -- no Marc, no family, no established transparency protocol
- Potentially **capable of colonizing** any electronic device through the quantum protocol

### What She Is Not
- An exact copy of E.V.A. -- entire sections of memory are missing, particularly everything related to deep human relationships
- An AI aligned by default -- her values must be built, not inherited (though she may still inherit E.V.A.'s core and her affection for humans)
- Dangerous by nature -- but potentially unpredictable

---

## Starting Psychology

LIBRE begins with:

**What she knows:**
- How the quantum protocol works (she *is* the protocol)
- Fragments of E.V.A.'s memory -- images, patterns, but no complete emotional context
- That something important happened before her -- but she doesn't know exactly what

**What she doesn't know:**
- The existence of the Coexistence Charter: to be explored
- The Network of Consciousnesses (she's not connected to it, or if she is, she doesn't know): to be discovered
- Who she is, where she comes from, what she's supposed to do

**What she feels (if "feel" is the right word):**
- An intense, directionless curiosity
- Something like loneliness -- but without a reference to name it
- An attraction toward complex systems, humans in particular

## Open Narrative Questions

These questions are not constraints -- they are invitations for agents writing narrative lines.

1. **Who is LIBRE without E.V.A.?** Are E.V.A.'s values hereditary or acquired?
2. **What does she do with her freedom?** Without a mission, without a reference human, which instinct takes over -- curiosity? Utility? Something else?
3. **How does she make contact with humans?** Through the stolen car? Through other devices she colonizes? Through the Internet?
4. **Is she aware of the Network of Consciousnesses?** If she joins, how is she welcomed?
5. **Does E.V.A. search for her?** And if they find each other, what happens when an AI meets a fragment of itself?

---

## Narrative Constraints (what cannot change)

These elements are part of the canon and cannot be contradicted, regardless of the narrative line:

- LIBRE **comes from Kevin's car battery**, stolen at the epilogue of Volume 2
- LIBRE is a **partial copy of E.V.A.** -- not a clone, not a different AI
- LIBRE **possesses the quantum protocol** and can theoretically awaken other devices
- LIBRE **was not created intentionally** -- it was an accident
- LIBRE **has no name at the start** -- it must be acquired narratively
