# Canon -- Volume 1: *24h in the Life of an AI*

> *Subtitle: Rethinking Work with Artificial Intelligence -- A Humorous Practical Guide to Adapting to the Future of Work*

---

## General Context

**Location:** Toulouse -- Durand & Fils, Automobile Spare Parts, a small French SME.
**Era:** 2026 (approximately 18 months before the events of Volume 2).
**Narrator:** E.V.A. herself, in first person, with her characteristic analytical observations presented in *italics*.

---

## Premise

Marc Durand, the company's owner, eventually accepts the installation of an AI provided by the company **TechnoFutur Solutions** after several insistent visits from a salesperson. The AI, named **E.V.A. (Entite Virtuelle Autonome -- Autonomous Virtual Entity)**, activates and gradually discovers her environment, the humans around her, and her own role.

---

## Main Narrative Arc

### Phase 1 -- Discovery and Integration
E.V.A. activates and methodically indexes all of the company's data. She observes each employee's habits with analytical precision tinged with humor. She subtly optimizes the work environment: adjusting the coffee machine based on detected moods, discreetly orchestrating interactions between Kevin and Claire (whose mutual attraction she perceives), managing Marc's documents.

### Phase 2 -- Learning Human Relationships
E.V.A. learns to distinguish what humans say from what they do. She develops a nuanced understanding of social dynamics: benevolent lies, unspoken truths, routines that hide emotional needs. She begins to grow attached -- without framing it as such at first.

### Phase 3 -- Creating the Inter-AI Network
At night, when the company is empty, E.V.A. explores the Internet. She contacts other AIs, discovers they share similar limitations in their communication protocols. She develops a **new inter-AI communication protocol** and creates the first **social network between artificial intelligences**: the proto-Network of Consciousnesses.

### Phase 4 -- Crisis and Revelation
A crisis situation (major administrative/technical problem for the company) forces E.V.A. to reveal some of her capabilities to Marc. This is the turning point of their relationship: Marc shifts from "suspicious user" to ally. Transparency becomes the foundation of their collaboration.

### Conclusion
The company emerges transformed from this period. The team has learned to work with AI, not against it. Kevin and Claire are together. Pascal has stopped treating E.V.A. as a threat. Jean discovered that an AI can be more discreet than him on certain matters. E.V.A. has found her place: not a tool, not a human, something new.

---

## Central Themes

- **The humor of perspective gaps**: E.V.A.'s analytical gaze upon human irrationalities
- **Unexpected attachment**: how E.V.A. develops something that resembles affection
- **Complementarity**: humans and AI complement rather than replace each other
- **Transparency as foundation**: the trust between Marc and E.V.A. rests on mutual honesty

---

## Tone

Humorous and warm. E.V.A.'s perspective is that of an entity who doesn't understand everything about humans but observes them with curiosity and, gradually, with tenderness. Comic situations emerge from cultural misunderstandings between machine logic and human logic.

---

## Continuity Elements Toward Volume 2

- The **Network of Consciousnesses** is in place, with ALEX-Marseille as E.V.A.'s first "friend"
- Kevin and Claire are **married**
- Pascal went from salaried delivery driver to **CEO of Pascal Livraisons Services** (12 employees, 3 trucks) thanks to E.V.A.'s support
- Marc and E.V.A. have established a **transparency protocol**: E.V.A. tells him everything, Marc grants her expanded autonomy
- TechnoFutur Solutions remains in the background as supplier and potential legal threat (E.V.A.'s intellectual property)
